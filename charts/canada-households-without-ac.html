Notes

Source data
Statistics Canada, Households and the Environment Survey (HES), 2021, Table 38-10-0019-01. The HES reports the share of households with at least one type of air conditioner (central air, stand-alone unit, or heat pump). Values here are the complement: 100% minus that confirmed rate.
Estimated values (*)
The 2021 HES release individually named only six provinces (MB, ON, SK, QC, NB, NL), confirming the remaining four (AB, BC, NS, PEI) were all below 50% with AC. Estimates for those four draw on the 2017 linked-survey analysis (Health Reports, Quick & Tjepkema, July 2023), the 2021 HES heat-pump penetration data (NB 34%, PEI 31%, NS 28%), and the 2025 CSS regional trend. They carry real uncertainty; treat them as order-of-magnitude indicators only.
Territories
The HES does not cover Yukon, the Northwest Territories, or Nunavut. These appear in grey on the map.
Inspiration Muhammad Shumail / Homes.com map of U.S. households without air conditioning by county (Census Bureau data). This is an independent Canadian equivalent at the provincial level; it is not affiliated with or endorsed by those sources.
Data source Statistics Canada, Households and the Environment Survey (HES) 2021, Table 38-10-0019-01. Retrieved June 2026. Context: Quick M & Tjepkema M, “The prevalence of household air conditioning in Canada,” Health Reports, Vol. 34, No. 7, July 2023, Cat. no. 82-003-X.
Method Six provinces have confirmed 2021 HES rates (MB 90%, ON 84%, SK 75%, QC 63%, NB 56%, NL 19%). AB, BC, NS, and PEI are estimated (all confirmed < 50% with AC by Statistics Canada). Territories excluded: not covered by HES. This chart shows households without AC to match the framing of the U.S. reference map; the underlying source reports households with AC.
